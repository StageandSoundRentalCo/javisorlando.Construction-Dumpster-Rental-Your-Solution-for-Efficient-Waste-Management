What Can Go in Your Construction Dumpster? Know Before You Throw

Not all waste is suitable for a construction dumpster. It’s essential to know what can and cannot be thrown in, especially when managing large amounts of debris. Here’s a breakdown of what you can dispose of in your construction dumpster rental:

Acceptable Materials for Your Dumpster:

Wood: Scraps of wood, timber, or panels from demolition or construction projects.

  • Concrete: Leftover concrete, cement, and debris from demolition work or new construction.
  • Drywall: Old drywall or plasterboard from renovations, repairs, or demolition.
  • Roofing Materials: Shingles, tiles, and other roofing debris.
  • Metal: Scrap metal like rebar, steel beams, and other metal components from construction sites.
  • Bricks & Stones: Leftover bricks, stones, and other building materials that may be removed during a demolition project.

What You Cannot Dispose of:

Hazardous Waste: Chemicals, paints, solvents, or any items that pose a health risk need to be handled separately and disposed of in accordance with local regulations.

Asbestos: If your construction project involves the removal of materials that may contain asbestos, it requires special handling and disposal through certified hazardous waste management services.

Tires and Batteries: These items must be recycled separately due to environmental concerns.

Appliances and Electronics: Many appliances and electronic items require specialized recycling and cannot be disposed of in regular construction dumpsters.

When in doubt, it’s best to consult your dumpster rental provider, like Javis Dumpster Rental Orlando, to ensure you’re following all local regulations and disposing of materials properly.
